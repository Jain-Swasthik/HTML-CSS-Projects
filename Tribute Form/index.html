Subhas Chandra Bose

The Unyielding Flame of India's Freedom

Here's a time line of Subhas Chandra Bose

  • 1897: Born on January 23 in Cuttack, Odisha, India.
  • 1916: Enrolled in the University of Calcutta; becomes actively involved in nationalist movements.
  • 1920: Joined the Indian National Congress (INC) and was influenced by leaders like Gandhi.
  • 1921: Elected as the President of the Students’ Union at the University of Calcutta.
  • 1938: Elected President of the Indian National Congress; emphasized the need for complete independence from British rule.
  • 1940: Resigned from INC due to differences with Gandhi and the leadership regarding the direction of the freedom movement.
  • 1941: Escaped from house arrest in India and traveled to Germany to seek support for India’s independence.
  • 1943: Became the Supreme Commander of the Indian National Army (INA) in Southeast Asia.
  • 1944: Launched a campaign against British forces in India, promoting armed struggle for independence.
  • 1945: Attended the Azad Hind Government, established in exile, to promote Indian independence.
  • 1945: Died in an airplane crash on August 18, under disputed circumstances, while traveling to form alliances for India's freedom.
